# [DRAFT] What is Office? — Products Should Keep Disrupting Themselves to Survive

**Source**: *Hit Refresh* — Satya Nadella (2017) + Office evolution as case study
**Research**: Mar02 Daily Research
**Image**: Satya Nadella at AI Tour — "What is Office, right?"

## Post Option A: Office as Hook → General Wisdom (RECOMMENDED)

```
"What is Office?"

In 30 years, Office has been:

◆ A desktop bundle: Word, Excel, PowerPoint
◆ A subscription: O365
◆ A collaboration layer: Teams as a container
◆ An AI interface: Copilot as the front door?

Same product. Four completely different identities.

Each time, Microsoft had to disrupt itself before someone else did.

That's the lesson — not just for Office.
For every product.

The companies that survive platform shifts
don't pivot when forced.

They keep asking the uncomfortable question
long before the crisis:

"What is this product, actually?"

◆ Learn — before signals become threats
◆ Question — what your product assumes about the world
◆ Stress-test — whether that identity still holds

Transformation is not an event. It is a practice.

Products should keep disrupting themselves to survive.

When did you ask that tough question?

#PM
```

## Post Option B: Punchy / Breakdown Style

```
"What is Office, right?"

Satya Nadella asked this — about his own product —
at the Microsoft AI Tour.

Not a typo. Not a joke.

Office has changed identity 4 times in 35 years:

1990 → A bundle of 3 desktop apps
2011 → A cloud subscription (Office 365)
2017 → A collaboration platform (Teams)
2025 → An AI interface (Copilot is the front door now)

Same product. Four different answers to the same question.

This is not accidental.

It's a strategy.

Nadella calls it "Hit Refresh" — treating transformation
not as a crisis response,
but as a designed rhythm:

◆ Disrupt yourself — before the market does
◆ Question your product's identity — regularly
◆ Stress-test what you actually are — every cycle

"Transformation is not an event. It is a practice."

The products that survive aren't the ones
that hold their identity the longest.

They're the ones brave enough to keep redefining it.

What is your product, right now?
And when was the last time you asked?

#ProductManagement #Leadership #AI
```
