# [DRAFT] PM as Creator — Marty Cagan's Warning

**Source**: Daily Digest 2026-02-20 (Marty Cagan, SVPG + Anthropic deskilling research)

---

## Post Option A: Breakdown Style (RECOMMENDED)

```
Marty Cagan doesn't say "PM as builder."
He says "PM as creator."

The distinction matters.

Builder = uses AI tools to make things.
Creator = owns whether it's WORTH making.

Cagan's framework — the PM owns 2 risks:

◆ Value — will customers choose to buy this?
◆ Viability — does it work for sales, legal, finance?

That's the job. Not PRDs. Not backlogs. Not Jira.

"It is not hard to create user stories or prioritize a backlog.
It IS hard to discover solutions worth building."
— Marty Cagan, SVPG

Here's the uncomfortable part:
AI makes the easy stuff trivial.
The hard stuff? More important than ever.

Still managing backlogs? Or discovering what's worth building?

#PM #AI
```

---

## Post Option B: Hot Take + Data Style

```
AI makes you 50% more productive.
And 17% less skilled.

Anthropic studied their own engineers.
The finding? Developers using AI scored 17% lower
on mastery quizzes — especially debugging.

They feel "full-stack."
But breadth came at the cost of depth.

Now apply this to PMs.

Every PM has access to Bolt, Cursor, v0.
Everyone can build a prototype in minutes.

So what's YOUR edge?

Marty Cagan calls it "product sense":

◆ Customer knowledge — how they buy, not just use
◆ Data knowledge — usage + business impact
◆ Market knowledge — competitive landscape
◆ Business knowledge — sales, legal, compliance constraints
◆ Product knowledge — operational expertise

AI can't build this for you.
Skip it, and you get the illusion of creation
without the substance.

Building with AI — or building product sense?

#PM #AI
```

## Post Option C: Personal Take Style

```
I've been tracking the "PM as builder" trend.

LinkedIn renamed APM → "Associate Product Builder."
Meta PMs rebranding as "AI builders."
Every newsletter says PMs must code.

Then I read Marty Cagan's latest.

He doesn't say "builder." He says "creator."

And he splits PMs into two camps:

🟢 Product Creators — own value + viability decisions
→ Market rewarding them. Salaries rising.

🔴 Non-Creators — backlog admins, facilitators, PM-titled project managers
→ "Increasingly vulnerable with the rise of AI."

His words, not mine.

The irony:
AI tools give everyone the ABILITY to build.
That makes judgment — not tools — the real differentiator.

The question isn't "can you build?"
It's "do you know what's worth building?"

You tell me.

#PM #AI #ProductManagement
```
