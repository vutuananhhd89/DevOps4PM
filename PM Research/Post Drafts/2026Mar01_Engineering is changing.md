## Discussion Questions to Develop This Topic Further

1. **Identity crisis**: If both PMs and engineers converge to "builder," what distinguishes them? Domain expertise? Customer access? Technical depth?
2. **Career paths**: How do engineering ladders change if "decide what to build" becomes part of every engineer's job?
3. **PM survival**: If engineers absorb PM tasks, is the standalone PM role justified at smaller companies?
4. **The Cagan connection**: Marty Cagan's "PM as creator" (your Feb 20 post) + Cherny's "engineer as builder" — are they describing the same person from opposite sides?
5. **Skills gap**: Which PM skills do engineers most struggle with? (customer empathy, stakeholder management, market sense?) Which engineering skills do PMs struggle with? (systems thinking, debugging, architecture?)
6. **Team structure**: Does the PM:engineer ratio (Andrew Ng's thesis) change if every engineer is partly a PM?
7. **Who gets cut?** If roles merge, which title survives: PM or engineer? Or does "builder" actually win?
