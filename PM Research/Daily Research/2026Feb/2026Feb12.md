## 💡 The Core Insight

The last five digests told a clear story:

- **Feb 8-9:** AI agents are transforming how we build
- **Feb 10:** The bottleneck shifted from engineering to PM — deciding *what* to build
- **Feb 11:** Spec quality is the new PM superpower

But there's a missing piece: **How do you discover what to build in the first place — faster?**

Traditional discovery (user interviews, surveys, competitive analysis) takes **weeks**. In an AI-accelerated world where engineering ships in **days**, slow discovery becomes the real bottleneck.

**The answer:** AI-first product discovery — using AI to compress research cycles from weeks to hours, without losing the human insight that makes discovery valuable.

---

## 🔬 The Discovery Speed Gap

| Phase | Old Speed | AI-Accelerated Speed | Gap Risk |
|-------|-----------|---------------------|----------|
| **User Interviews** | 2-4 weeks to recruit + conduct | AI synthesizes past interviews in minutes | Losing nuance |
| **Feedback Analysis** | 1-2 weeks to tag and theme | AI processes thousands of inputs instantly | Missing context |
| **Competitive Research** | 3-5 days manual review | AI monitors competitors in real-time | Over-reliance on surface data |
| **Hypothesis Generation** | Team brainstorm (half-day) | AI suggests hypotheses from data patterns | Confirmation bias |
| **Prototype Testing** | 1-2 weeks to build and test | AI + synthetic users test in hours | False confidence |

> **The paradox:** Engineering now ships in days. If discovery still takes weeks, you're either shipping without discovery (dangerous) or slowing down to match it (wasteful). AI-first discovery closes this gap.

## ⚠️ Teresa Torres' Warning: Don't Fall Into "Discovery Theater"

Teresa Torres — the leading voice on continuous discovery — has a critical warning for PMs racing to AI-first discovery:

### What Discovery Theater Looks Like

| 🚩 Discovery Theater | ✅ Real Discovery |
|-----------------------|-------------------|
| AI summarizes interviews → PM skips reading them | AI surfaces patterns → PM verifies with real conversations |
| Synthetic users replace all customer contact | Synthetic users **pre-test** → real users **validate** |
| AI generates insights → PM presents them as their own | AI accelerates synthesis → PM applies **judgment and context** |
| "We used AI for research" = checkbox | AI tools embedded in a **continuous** discovery habit |
| Data-rich, empathy-poor | Data-informed, empathy-driven |

### Torres' Rules for AI-Augmented Discovery

1. **AI is a thought partner, not a replacement** — Use it to extend your capacity, not skip the work
2. **Never remove humans from the loop** — AI summaries miss nuance; direct customer conversations remain your edge
3. **Ground synthetic insights in real data** — Synthetic users are only as good as the data they're trained on
4. **Story-based interviewing > hypothetical questions** — AI can't replicate the insight from hearing a customer's actual experience
5. **Evals ARE a discovery habit** — For AI products, evaluation workflows map directly to assumption testing

> "Removing humans from the discovery loop doesn't save time — it creates expensive blind spots."
> — Teresa Torres

## 🧩 Synthetic Users: Promise and Peril

### What Synthetic Users Are

AI-generated user profiles that simulate the behaviors, needs, and reactions of real users. They can:

- Test prototypes and identify usability issues
- Provide rapid survey responses at scale
- Simulate specific scenarios (edge cases, accessibility, etc.)
- Pre-validate concepts before real user testing

### When to Use (and When NOT to)

| ✅ Use Synthetic Users For | ❌ Don't Use Them For |
|---------------------------|----------------------|
| Pre-testing usability of wireframes | Replacing all customer interviews |
| Stress-testing edge cases at scale | Discovering genuinely new needs |
| Getting quick directional feedback | Making high-stakes product bets |
| Filling gaps between real research rounds | Understanding emotional motivations |
| Accessibility and performance testing | Building empathy with real people |

> **Rule of thumb:** Synthetic users for **breadth and speed**. Real users for **depth and truth**.
