## 0. My Writing Voice

### Tone & Style

| Trait | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Confident but humble** | Share opinions boldly, but acknowledge personal struggles ("almost failed with 2 below targets") |
| **Direct & punchy** | Short sentences. No fluff. Get to the point. |
| **Conversational** | Use dashes (—) for asides, "You tell me" endings |
| **Authority + credibility** | Quote experts/sources (Jeff Sutherland), cite data (60,000+ teams) |

### Structure Pattern

```
[Bold hook / provocative statement]

[Brief context or attribution]

[Bullet list with colored emojis: 🚀📦🎯]
  - Keep bullets SHORT
  - Use em-dashes for explanations

[Data/proof point if available]

[Rhetorical question or "You tell me"]

[Hashtags: 2-4 max, at the end]
```

### Emoji Usage

- ✅ Use sparingly: 🚀 📦 🎯 💪 🙏 😅 :D
- ✅ Colored square bullets: 🟢 🟡 or ◆
- ❌ Avoid overuse – you're not a hype account

### Signature Patterns

- **Quote authority**: "Jeff Sutherland's words. Legend."
- **Challenge the reader**: "Still guessing hours? Or have you made the leap?"
- **End with question**: "Is it still worth it in 2025? You tell me."
- **Personal vulnerability**: Share struggles, near-failures, lessons

### Hashtags You Use

`#NoEstimate` `#PMLegend` `#PMP` `#PgMP` `#DevOps`

### What NOT to Do

- ❌ Long paragraphs
- ❌ Corporate jargon
- ❌ Humble-bragging without substance
- ❌ Generic motivational quotes

## 4. Daily Research → Post Drafts Workflow

### Step 1: Daily Research

**Goal:** Find 1 insightful article from a reliable source and deep-dive into it.

**Reliable Sources (Prioritized):**

| Tier | Source | Why It's Reliable |
|------|--------|-------------------|
| **1 – Gold** | Lenny's Newsletter, Marty Cagan (SVPG), Reforge | Industry-leading PM thinkers, practitioner-based |
| **1 – Gold** | a]6z, First Round Review, Stratechery | VC/founder perspectives with depth |
| **2 – Strong** | The Pragmatic Engineer, TLDR Tech | Technical depth, engineering context |
| **2 – Strong** | Mind the Product, ProductPlan | PM community, case studies |
| **2026Feb11** | Spec-driven development, vibe coding vs spec coding, executable specs for AI agents |
| **2026Feb12** | AI-first product discovery, synthetic users, continuous AI-augmented discovery |
| **3 – Monitor** | Medium (specific authors), Substack PMs | Check credibility per author |

**Daily Research Structure:**

```
# Daily Research – [Date]

## 🔥 Deep Dive: [Article Title]

**Source:** [Author/Publication] — [Date]

> [Key quote from the article]

---

## 💡 The Core Insight
[1-2 paragraphs: What's the main thesis?]

---

## [Framework/Model sections with tables]
[Extract actionable frameworks, checklists, models]

---

## ✅ Action Items
[What can a PM do this week based on this?]

---

## 🎯 Post-Worthy Ideas
[Table of angles and hooks for LinkedIn posts]
```

**Quality Checklist for Daily Research:**

- [ ] Is the source a recognized expert, not just a thought-piece?
- [ ] Does it include a framework, model, or actionable advice?
- [ ] Can I extract 2-3 post hooks from this?
- [ ] Does it connect to current PM trends (AI, agents, specs, etc.)?

**⚠️ Research Quality Rule:**

> **Always deep-dive, never skim.** A Daily Research must go deep into **1 strong Tier 1–2 source** — read the full article, extract the author's actual framework, and synthesize practitioner-level insights. Broad overviews that list 5 surface-level signals without reading any source are **not helpful for PMs** and should be avoided. If the research feels shallow (no direct quotes, no framework extracted, no actionable takeaways), go back and read the source properly before writing the daily research.

**Deep vs. Shallow Test:**

| ❌ Shallow (avoid) | ✅ Deep (target) |
|--------------------|-----------------|
| Lists 4–5 signals from search results | Reads 1 article in full, extracts the author's core argument |
| No direct quotes from the source | Includes key quotes with context |
| Generic takeaways ("AI is changing PM!") | Specific frameworks, models, or checklists from the source |
| Could be written without reading any article | Could NOT be written without reading the source |
